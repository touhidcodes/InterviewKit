# MongoDB Sharding (Interview Q&A)

## 1. What is sharding?
Sharding is a method of distributing data across multiple servers.

---

## 2. Why is sharding used?
To handle large datasets and scale horizontally.

---

## 3. What are the main components of sharding?

- Shards  
- Config servers  
- Query routers (mongos)  

---

## 4. What is a shard?
A shard is a MongoDB server that stores a portion of the data.

---

## 5. What is a shard key?
A shard key determines how data is distributed across shards.

---

## 6. What are the types of sharding?

- Range-based sharding  
- Hash-based sharding  

---

## 7. What is chunk?
A chunk is a subset of data within a shard.

---

## 8. What is balancing?
Redistributing data evenly across shards.

---

## 9. What are the benefits?

- Horizontal scalability  
- Improved performance  
- Load distribution  

---

## 10. What are the challenges?

- Complex setup  
- Choosing correct shard key  

---

## Summary

Sharding enables MongoDB to scale efficiently for large applications.
